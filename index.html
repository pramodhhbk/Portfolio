  • Pressing Intensity

    Dec, 2024

    Bayer Leverkusen Pressing Intensity

    Bayer Leverkusen’s Pressing Masterclass Against Bayern Munich 3-0 Victory Bayer Leverkusen’s emphatic 3-0 win over Bayern Munich in February 2024 showcased Xabi Alonso’s tactical brilliance and pressing efficiency.


    Using PPDA (Passes Per Defensive Action) as a metric, this analysis highlights how Leverkusen’s structured pressing disrupted Bayern’s buildup play and limited their attacking threat across all pitch areas. PPDA is a widely used metric in football analytics to measure a team's pressing intensity and defensive aggression. It evaluates how many passes a team allows its opponent to complete before making a defensive action to regain possession.


    A low PPDA signifies aggressive pressing, aimed at quickly regaining possession, while a high PPDA reflects a more passive, space-controlling approach. In the Defensive Third, Tapsoba spearheaded Leverkusen’s intense pressing (PPDA: 2.04), cutting out threats early. In the Middle Third, the team balanced pressure and shape with a PPDA of 7.08, where Hincapié played a key role.


    In the Offensive Third, Leverkusen shifted to a more controlled press (PPDA: 8.56), relying on Wirtz and Xhaka to apply pressure higher up the field. This visualization and analysis were created using Python, mplsoccer, Matplotlib, and Pandas. They demonstrate how data-driven insights can highlight the finer details of tactical setups.

    La Liga Shots Dashboard

    I recently came across a fantastic video by McKay Johns where he recreated an Athletic post analyzing Erling Haaland's shots in the Premier League 2023-24. Inspired by this, I created a similar visualization using Tableau and Python, but with a twist!


    This time, I focused on La Liga, using data from Understat and Opta to build a dynamic dashboard that visualizes and analyzes all the shots taken by the top 6 goalscorers from last season. This dashboard highlights which positions these players converted their chances from and provides insights into the expected goals (xG) per shot taken.


    Key features include:
    - Average Distance: The dashboard shows the average distance in yards from all the shots taken by the players.
    - Shot Sources: Tabs to analyze shots from various sources, such as left foot, right foot, and headers.
    - Performance Highlights: Last season's Golden Boot winner (Pichichi Trophy) Artem Dovbyk surpassed his total xG; notably, every player excelled in terms of Goals vs xG.


    I kicked things off by writing some Python code to extract the Understat data, followed by preliminary data cleaning and feature engineering. The final product is a powerful tool for analysts and coaches to work with forwards, helping them identify strengths and weaknesses in shooting and positioning.

    As the summer transfer window closes, Chelsea Football Club needs a reliable No. 9. While Victor Osimhen remains their top target, his €11M salary clashes with Chelsea’s new performance-based wage structure. Enter Ivan Toney from Brentford as a smart alternative

    Player Spotlight: Marc Cucurella at Euro 2024

    This post is part of a series of soccer analytics using Statsbomb Data, analyzed with Python and Tableau.


    Marc Cucurella has shown remarkable performance at Euro 2024, playing 6 matches with 546 minutes and maintaining a 92.17% passing accuracy. He has executed 8 tackles, recovered 34 balls, and covered a distance of 63.1 km. Notably, his top speed was 33.1 km/h. Cucurella’s pass success rate highlights his efficiency across different zones, particularly excelling in the defensive and middle thirds


    This stellar performance reflects his transformation under Mauricio Pochettino, where he hybridized his role as an inverted full-back, playing as an additional No. 6 alongside Caicedo towards the end of the Premier League season. This strategic shift was pivotal for Chelsea, contributing to their winning streak in the league's final matches.


    Cucurella provided the Euro title-winning assist against England in the finals. He also had a great Euro season alongside teammate Rodri, with many involvements throughout the tournament.


    Enzo Maresca, the current Chelsea Manager, is trying to invert either Reece James or Malo Gusto on the right. However, if he wants a player who already has experience playing as an inverted full-back, just like Reece James, then Marc Cucurella is the man he needs to go to.


    One area for improvement is his tackling. Cucurella occasionally overcommits forward, leaving him exposed and necessitating frequent tracking back. This sometimes exposes the center-backs, making them vulnerable to counter-attacks from the right side. Working on timing his tackles better and maintaining positional discipline will help mitigate these risks.
